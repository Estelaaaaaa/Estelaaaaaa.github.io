How to learn music theory?

Basically, every piece of music consists of two basic things: pitch and rhythm. Learning how these concepts work and build upon themselves is essential for mastering the theory. The best music theory courses cover basic and advanced concepts and use simple language and helpful diagrams or audio examples to teach. Here are the top things you should expect in any music theory course material:
1) Pitch training (they explain topics such as scales, intervals, chords, inversions, keys, harmony, etc.)
2) Rhythmic training (duration, rhythmic structures and tempo, rhythmic symbols and values)
3) Composition (relying on both notes and rhythm, and combining them to create music)
4) Ear training (to be able to identify things like intervals, chord types, modulations and rhythm by ear, just by listening to them and not looking at notes)

What are the rules to volleyball?

Volleyball is an Olympic sport with a ball in which two opposing teams play on a field with a net stretched in the middle. The object of the game is to send the ball over the net to touch the opponent's court and prevent the opponent from doing the same. The team has the right to three plays (not counting the touch of the block), before returning the ball to the opponent.

The ball is put into play by service: the serving player directs the ball over the net towards the opponent. The game continues until the ball touches the field, goes out of the field (out), and if the team fails to return it in the permitted manner. The team that wins the rally wins a point (Rally Point System). When the team receiving the serve wins the rally, it wins a point, and thus acquires the right to serve, and the players rotate one place clockwise.
A point is scored when a successfully sent ball touches the opponent's field, when the opposing team makes a mistake and when the opposing team receives a penalty. Rallying is a series of actions in the game from the moment of the service shot by the server to the moment when the ball is out of play. The serving team wins the rally wins the point and continues to serve, and if the team receiving the serve wins the rally, wins the point and must serve next.
The winner of the match is the team that wins three sets, and in the case of a tied score of 2-2, the decisive fifth set is played to 15 points with a difference of at least two points.

Why did Van Gogh cut off his ear?

The first theory

Historians state that the trigger for such a decision was most likely the news that his brother had become engaged. According to one study, Van Gogh learned this news on the very day he cut off his ear, December 23, 1888. Experts believe that this knowledge surprised and frightened Van Gogh that he would remain forever alone, without money, that is, without the financial support of his brother, who enabled him to live the life he led. The news upset him greatly and caused him to commit self-harm. His brother's marriage may not have been the key reason, but it did initiate and reinforce his otherwise destructive behavior.

Another theory

The next theory about Vincent van Gogh's ear, according to some sources, begins on this day in 1888, when van Gogh was in the company of his friend, the French artist Paul Gauguin. They got into a big argument, and as a result of this argument, in order to prove some of his claims, van Gogh cut off his ear. After that, he went to a nearby brothel where he gave a girl his severed ear wrapped in a cloth and asked her to look after him. The girl fainted from the shock, and Van Gogh returned home and went to sleep. When the police arrived, he admitted that he had cut off his own ear and that it was his own fault.


What are performing arts?

Performance has become one of the key terms of the new century. Traditionally, this term is associated with artistic practices that have adopted certain theatrical and ceremonial techniques and approaches. Performance practice was deconstructed by artists, from the second half of the 20th century onwards, who were interested in breaking down theatrical and artistic rules. They devised new ways of shocking the audience based on a live and direct approach, as well as a unique, one-time presentation of works of art. The uniqueness of the work and the direct relationship with the audience were crucial for the first generation of new performance artists.
Today it is better to use the term "performance art" instead of "performance art". The reason for this is that, in a narrower sense, the strict meaning of performance has been replaced with multidimensional, hybrid ways of understanding and practicing this genre. Such a redefinition was achieved primarily thanks to the transmedia way of working, the one that connected the original meaning of the performance with other technologies and media and resulted from a direct relationship with the audience. On the other hand, , the concept gained importance partly thanks to the proliferation and expansion of the concept of "performativeness", which emphasized questions related to the mediation and spheres of art, rather than the question of theatricality itself.


What are the best art museums in the world?

1. Louvre Museum – Paris

It is located in the center of Paris, on the banks of the Seine River, and is one of the world's largest museums. This museum contains one of the richest art collections in the world. It houses more than 35,000 works of art displayed on 60,600 m², and it is estimated that it would take visitors about five weeks to properly view all the works. The most famous work of art exhibited in the Louvre is Da Vinci's painting Mona Lisa.

2. The British Museum – London

The British Museum is one of the most important museums in the world and contains collections of the history and culture of mankind. The collection of about 7 million exhibits from all continents is among the largest and most comprehensive in the world. The museum is unique in the world, because it has two institutions under the same roof; the national museum of antiquities, and the national library.

3. Smithsonian Institution – Washington

It is the world's largest museum and research complex, consisting of 19 museums and galleries, the National Zoological Park and nine research facilities.

How do I start sowing?

At home, find materials (old clothes, bedspreads, curtains, etc.) that you will not regret if you destroy them, or buy cheaper materials that are very easy to handle, for example muslin. Start with simple projects such as pillowcases, curtains, aprons, trouser hems, anything that requires minimal tailoring and includes simple straight seams, or hemming. When you "get to know" your sewing machine, and gain the first skills and confidence, you can start on more complicated projects, which include more complex tailoring and sewing.

As for tailoring, it can be said that it is another separate skill, which you will have to master (with the help of books, tutorials).

Before you decide to buy a few meters of expensive delicate material and sew a formal dress, we recommend that you get a garment made of the same or similar material in a second-hand store, and try to remake that garment to a certain extent. In this way, you will learn how certain materials behave, and reduce the possibility of mistakes, which you will not be able to correct on a new, expensive material.
